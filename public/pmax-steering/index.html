How to Properly Steer Performance Max Campaigns

By Ronald B. Smith • April 11, 2026 • 11 min read

PMax isn't a hands-off channel. It's a powerful machine that needs a skilled operator. Here's the exact framework we use to steer it toward real profit.

Why PMax needs an operator, not just a launch

Performance Max consolidates Shopping, Display, YouTube, Search, Gmail, Discover, and Maps into one campaign type controlled by Google's AI. In theory it should find the best conversions across every channel automatically. In practice it has one dominant objective: spend your budget and hit whatever conversion target you set.

Without human steering, PMax will inflate ROAS by hoarding brand traffic, burn budget on junk placements, and ignore your most profitable products in favour of whatever converts fastest. Steering it means controlling the inputs — structure, signals, assets, negatives — so the algorithm's output actually maps to your profit goals.

1

Account structure: segment by purpose

Don't run one PMax campaign for everything. Segment by business logic:

  • → By product category — so budget allocation reflects actual margin tiers, not blended averages
  • → By margin tier — high-margin products deserve more aggressive tROAS targets and more budget
  • → New customer vs. remarketing — separate new customer acquisition from retention to measure true CAC
  • → Brand vs. non-brand — run brand in a separate Search campaign and use exclusions in PMax to protect visibility
2

Audience signals: don't launch cold

Audience signals tell Google's algorithm where to start looking. The machine will eventually explore beyond them, but a strong signal list cuts weeks off the learning phase.

Customer Match

Upload your buyer list. Segment by LTV where possible.

Remarketing lists

Website visitors, cart abandoners, past purchasers — all of them.

Custom segments

Based on your top converting search terms and competitor site visits.

3

Asset quality: variety and relevance

PMax needs enough creative diversity to serve across all placements. Thin asset groups — one headline, one image — force the algorithm to recycle the same creative endlessly and degrade performance. Aim for:

  • → 15 headlines covering product benefit, category, brand, and urgency angles
  • → 5 descriptions that speak to different objections or use cases
  • → A mix of square, landscape, and portrait images — real product photography, not stock
  • → At least one short-form video (15–30s) even if it's a simple product slideshow

Review asset performance ratings monthly. Replace "Low" rated assets systematically — don't wait for the algorithm to fix it.

4

Negatives & placement exclusions

PMax doesn't expose search terms the way standard Search campaigns do, but you can still apply account-level negative keyword lists. Populate these with irrelevant categories, competitor names you don't want to associate with, and known junk terms from your search campaigns.

Separately, pull your placement report monthly and exclude low-quality Display and YouTube placements — mobile app categories (especially kids' apps and games) are notorious for wasting budget.

Important note

Brand exclusions require a support request to Google. Submit this before launch to prevent PMax from cannibalising your branded Search traffic and inflating ROAS with easy brand conversions.

5

Brand protection: run brand in Search

Always run a parallel brand Search campaign with exact and phrase match on your brand terms. This gives you visibility and control over your brand SERP independent of PMax, ensures competitors can't dominate your brand name, and keeps brand ROAS metrics clean and separate from non-brand acquisition metrics.

6

Reporting & iteration cadence

PMax needs a minimum 6-week learning window before you make major structural changes. But during that window, you're not passive:

  • → Weekly: check search term insights, asset ratings, and budget pacing
  • → Monthly: review placement exclusions, refresh audience signals, replace low-rated assets
  • → Quarterly: re-evaluate campaign structure, tROAS targets, and product segmentation

Common PMax mistakes to avoid

✗ Launching without brand exclusions — PMax will cannibalize your brand traffic and report inflated ROAS
✗ One asset group for all products — loses visibility into what's actually performing
✗ Changing bids or budgets every week — resets the learning phase repeatedly
✗ No audience signals at launch — wastes 2–4 weeks of budget on cold exploration
✗ Trusting Google's ROAS at face value — always cross-reference with backend data and actual margin
