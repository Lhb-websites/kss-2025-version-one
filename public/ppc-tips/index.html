10 Expert PPC Optimization Tips That Still Work in 2026

By Ronald B. Smith • April 11, 2026 • 9 min read

Automation didn't kill PPC strategy — it raised the stakes. Here are ten fundamentals that separate profitable accounts from money pits in 2026.

On this page

01

Separate brand from non-brand — always

Mixing brand and non-brand in the same campaign inflates ROAS and hides poor performance on acquisition terms. Run brand in its own campaign with a separate budget and bidding strategy. This gives you clear visibility into what's actually growing your customer base versus capturing existing demand.

02

Audit your negative keywords every week

One of the fastest ways to reduce wasted spend. Pull your search terms report weekly, flag anything irrelevant or cannibalising brand, and add negatives at the campaign or account level. Most accounts have 20–40% of spend going to junk terms that a proper negative list would have blocked. This is especially critical after enabling Performance Max.

03

Segment campaigns by purchase intent

Top-of-funnel awareness terms, mid-funnel comparison terms, and bottom-of-funnel buy-now terms behave completely differently. Grouping them together means your bids and budgets serve a blended average that wins nothing. Segment campaigns by intent tier and let each find its own efficient CPA.

04

Feed PMax strong audience signals

PMax learns from signals — customer match lists, remarketing audiences, and custom segments based on search intent. Without them it starts cold and wastes weeks of budget. Upload your buyer list, build custom segments around your top converting search terms, and add your remarketing lists before you launch. The machine learns faster with better inputs.

05

Track contribution margin, not just ROAS

Google's reported ROAS ignores COGS, shipping, returns, and platform fees. A 5x ROAS on a product with 20% margins is losing money. Build a simple margin tracker — even a spreadsheet — that maps ad spend to gross profit. Use this as your true north, and set tROAS targets based on what margin you actually need, not what looks good in the dashboard.

06

Test RSA headlines in isolation

Don't pin all 15 headlines and call it done. Pin one or two headlines to control positions, then rotate test variants in the remaining slots. Use the asset performance report to identify low-rated assets and replace them systematically. RSA optimisation is an ongoing process, not a one-time setup.

07

Use asset group segmentation in PMax

One asset group for everything is the most common PMax mistake. Segment by product category, margin tier, or audience type. This gives you control over which assets serve which products, allows smarter budget allocation, and makes it possible to isolate what's actually performing versus what the algorithm is favouring.

08

Leverage first-party data every quarter

Upload fresh customer match lists at least quarterly. Segment by LTV — high-value buyers, one-time buyers, lapsed customers. Use these to bid more aggressively on look-alike segments and suppress irrelevant audiences. First-party data is your moat against the deprecation of third-party signals.

09

Monitor search impression share on brand terms

If competitors are running on your brand terms and your impression share drops below 90%, you're ceding your cheapest conversions. Keep a weekly eye on brand impression share and defend it aggressively — it's almost always the highest-ROAS traffic in the account.

10

Review PMax placement reports monthly

PMax can serve ads across Gmail, YouTube, Display, Discover, and Maps. Many placements drive zero-intent clicks that burn budget. Pull the placement report monthly, exclude low-quality sites and apps, and use placement-level data to inform your asset strategy. This single step often recovers 10–20% of wasted spend.
